# The Best Ways to Reduce Your Carbon Footprint

We hear a lot about our carbon footprint. But what is it? And how can we reduce it?

A person's carbon footprint is the amount of greenhouse gases they produce from the things they do every day.

It's difficult to know how much of these gases we produce. But trying to reduce them is good for the environment, and it could also save you money!

One thing we can do is reduce the amount of time we spend in our cars. When you can, it's better for the environment for you to walk, not drive.

And if you really do need to drive, you could think about sharing a car with other people.

You could also buy food that didn't travel very much to get to you. Some of our food travels from other countries to our shops — and all that travel isn't good for the environment.

So you could buy food that's grown close to your home. Or you could grow some food in your garden, if you have one!

Eating less meat is better for the environment, too. Farm animals produce a lot of greenhouse gases. So you can reduce your carbon footprint by having less meat, and more fruits and vegetables.

These are changes we can make ourselves. But if you want to make bigger changes, you could also think about voting for political parties that want to help the environment!
