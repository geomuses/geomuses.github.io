# One-Third of Phone Time Is Mindless Scrolling — Report

Have you ever picked up your phone to look at the time, check the weather or read a message — only to realize 30 minutes have passed?

If this sounds familiar, you're not alone.

A report by Virgin Media O2 has found that a large amount of the time people spend on their phones is spent mindlessly scrolling through apps and social media. And a quick check can turn into minutes — or even hours — of mindless scrolling.

Researchers surveyed 6,000 people aged over 16 in the UK, and found that they spend around four hours a day on their smartphones. And more than one-third of that time may be spent without a clear purpose.

Experts say the way smartphones are designed makes it difficult to stop mindlessly scrolling. There are new videos, posts and notifications all the time, and before long, people may forget why they picked up their phones in the first place.

The report also found that people who spend more time scrolling without a purpose are more likely to have negative experiences online. Some people said they felt worse after using their phones, while others said they saw unpleasant or upsetting content.

So what can we do about it?

Experts say it's important to just notice your habits. By paying attention to when and why you pick up your phone, you may find it easier to stay in control of your screen time.

But mindless scrolling isn't always a bad thing, and many people use their phones to relax, have fun, or take a break after a busy day.

However, if a quick check often turns into an hour of scrolling, it may be a good idea to ask whether that time is helping you feel better, or making you feel worse.
