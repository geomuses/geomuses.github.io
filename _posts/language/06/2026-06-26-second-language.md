# Would-be Ninjas Sit Written Test in Japan

When we think of ninja, we probably imagine spies and assassins all dressed in black, creeping over rooftops, throwing their star-shaped shuriken — and then disappearing into the night.

We probably don't imagine them sitting down to take a written exam.

However, that's exactly what happened in the city of Koka in Shiga prefecture on June 14, when about 130 would-be ninjas took part in the Koka-ryu Ninja Certification Exam.

Koka is the historical home of the Koka-ryu school of ninjutsu — the martial art of the ninja. Today there are several attractions in the city that celebrate this ninja history, such as the Koka Ninjutsu Museum, the Koka Ninja Village theme park and the Koka Ninja House — which was once the home of real ninja!

And for the last 18 years, a research group in Koka has organized an exam for would-be ninjas.

The exam tests people's knowledge of ninja history and culture, as well as the tools and weapons that ninja used. Those who pass the test receive hand-scroll certificates at beginner, intermediate and advanced levels.

This year, 92 people took the beginner test, and 28 took the intermediate test. Up to 10 could take the advanced test.

All the test-takers took a written exam, but at the beginner level, they could also get points for their costume and for their skill at throwing a shuriken.

Nearly 95% of the beginners passed, but fewer than 60% passed the intermediate test. The results of the advanced test have not yet been announced, but last year five out of nine test-takers passed.

People traveled from all over Japan as well as from places like Singapore and Hong Kong to take this year's test. The oldest test-taker was 76 years old and the youngest was 9.

So it seems that the test is helping to keep ninja culture alive — and who knows? Maybe there is an officially certified ninja somewhere near you right now!
