# Should Restaurants Have 'Adults Only' Areas?

You've been planning a romantic evening for weeks. But when the night finally comes and you sit down together in the restaurant, there are children running around and shouting at the next table.

Would that ruin your evening?

Some people think it might. And according to a new survey, most Americans think restaurants should offer at least some kind of "adults only" dining.

The survey, done by a company called Lightspeed Commerce, found that 75% of US adults support the idea of adults-only dining to some extent. And that number goes up to 79% among parents!

However, many said it depends on the type of restaurant and the time of day.

The most popular idea was keeping children out of restaurants late at night. Many people also thought restaurants should have some adults-only areas, or spaces reserved for romantic meals between adults.

Many also said children shouldn't be in places that focus on serving alcohol.

These aren't new ideas, of course. There are already cafes and restaurants around the world that are only for adults, or that don't let children in after certain times.

And people who don't want to sit near children might at least try to ask for "a quiet table."

Some say it's not just children who are the problem — writing on Reddit, one user said rude and loud people of any age shouldn't be allowed in restaurants!

Americans may actually be happier eating with pets — because 45% of people in the survey said they think dogs should be allowed in restaurants, at least in outside areas!
