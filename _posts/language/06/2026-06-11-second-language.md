# How to Apologize at Work — And Why It Matters

Here's something you've probably heard — or said — a lot: "We all make mistakes."

Making mistakes is a natural part of any job, but it's not always easy finding the right words to apologize for them.

So what does a good apology look like or sound like?

It needs to be specific, says leadership expert Morag Barrett. Explain exactly what you're apologizing for and don't make excuses or blame others.

But don't say too much, she adds — "Say it, mean it, be done with it." You don't need to apologize again and again.

Kat Boogaard from software company Atlassian says that you should then tell your boss or your colleagues how you can make sure you won't make the same mistake again.

In other words, tell others what steps you'll take to change a situation, or do something differently.

This shows them you know exactly what went wrong — and how to fix it.

Boogaard also says that a good apology helps to build trust. It shows the people you work with that you're able to take accountability for your actions. They'll also see that you are honest and you want to improve.

Apologizing could also help to improve the morale of the whole team by encouraging a culture of honesty and accountability.

However, it's important to remember that apologizing might not solve everything right away. Workplace expert Jason Harney says that other people may have been affected by your mistake and they may be angry.
