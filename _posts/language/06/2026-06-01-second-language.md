# S. Korea's National Parks Offer Free Safety Equipment

For some people, a mountain hike may sound like the perfect holiday activity. But in South Korea, officials say many tourists are getting hurt while hiking.

To help with this problem, visitors to any of the 20 mountainous parks run by the Korea National Park Service (KNPS) can now rent safety equipment for free.

Both South Korean and foreign visitors will be able to use nine types of equipment without having to pay for it. These include things like hiking poles and boots, first aid kits, maps and backpacks.

Before now, 12 national parks in South Korea already provided free equipment, but the KNPS added eight more parks to the list at the start of May.

In 2021, Bukhansan National Park, just outside Seoul, became the first park to start providing free equipment to visitors.

The idea was to try to reduce the number of accidents caused by people not having the right equipment. Over time, other parks joined the program.

The KNPS also plans to help foreign tourists by offering English-language brochures and signs at its parks.

Visitors can rent equipment from the visitor centers at any of the parks, after filling in a form with their information. Equipment can only be kept for one day.

Last year, there were about 6,600 equipment rentals at participating parks, according to _The Korea Times_. Hiking poles were the most popular piece of equipment to rent.
