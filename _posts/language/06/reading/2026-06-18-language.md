# AI Saves Doctors Time, Reduces Mistakes — Survey

From writing patient notes to helping look at scan results, AI is becoming a useful tool in hospitals around the world. And a new survey has found that AI may be helping doctors save time, see more patients and even avoid mistakes!

The survey by health technology company Philips included more than 2,000 healthcare workers from 10 countries. It found that AI use among healthcare workers is growing, with 65% saying they have increased their use of AI tools in the last year.

They say they use AI for daily tasks such as managing information, writing patient notes and organizing appointments — and almost half say they use AI as a professional "buddy" to discuss ideas with!

They also use AI to get warnings about dangerous drug combinations, suggest possible illnesses based on patients' symptoms, and help look at scan results.

And it seems to be having real effects. Forty-six percent of healthcare workers said AI saves them at least 132 hours each year — more than three full work weeks!

Because of this extra time, 50% said they can see more patients. On average, they said they were seeing eight extra patients each week.

Doctors also said AI is helping them do research and study difficult cases, and it's even helping them make better decisions.

In fact, 39% of healthcare workers said they had seen AI help find or prevent possible mistakes at least three times in the past three months.

Even so, most healthcare workers don't want AI making decisions on its own.

Ninety percent of healthcare workers believe human involvement is still important, and 86% say doctors should always review what AI is telling them before taking action.

AI may be changing healthcare, but most medical workers believe doctors should stay responsible for making important decisions and looking after patients. At least for now, many see AI as a helpful assistant, but not a replacement for human care.
