# Workers Spend 6.4 Hours a Week 'Botsitting' AI

We get told all the time how AI chatbots can help us with our work. Experts say they can improve productivity — though many of us also worry that they may replace us!

But according to a recent survey of 6,000 full-time office workers in the US, the UK and Australia, AI may still be a long way from replacing us — because it still needs us to "botsit" it!

"Botsit" is a word used by San Francisco-based AI company Glean in its report on the use of AI at work.

Botsitting is like babysitting for AI. It's all the work that people have to do after asking AI to do something for them — things like checking the AI's results, fixing wrong or incomplete answers, giving the AI more context, and running new prompts to get something more useful.

And according to Glean's survey, workers now spend an average of 6.4 hours a week botsitting.

That's more time than they spend just using AI — meaning they're spending more time cleaning up AI's work than they are using it to get things done.

And doing all that botsitting is making workers feel worn out. In fact, workers who spend 40% or more of their AI time botsitting are 73% more likely to be looking for a new job!

Some workers get so tired of botsitting that they stop trying. Sixty-nine percent of workers who use AI said they have submitted results from AI that they haven't checked, don't understand, or couldn't defend if asked.

However, not all botsitting is bad. Workers who say AI has helped increase their productivity actually spend the most time botsitting — but they are also more likely to choose not to use AI for tasks that they don't think will benefit from it.
