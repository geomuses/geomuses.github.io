# AI Could Help Small Businesses Survive — Survey

If you run a small business, you might have to think about who you need to hire to help you grow the company.

Perhaps you need someone to help with marketing, and someone to deal with customer service. Maybe you need another person to do the accounting and an employee to manage social media.

But according to a new survey, many small business owners end up doing all of those jobs themselves.

Survey company Talker Research spoke to 1,000 small business owners in the US.

More than half said they manage customer service, while 44% do marketing, 43% do accounting and more than four out of 10 are responsible for social media.

The problem is that not all owners feel qualified to do these jobs. And doing them means they have less time to run the business.

It also means that the average small business owner is working an extra 200 hours per year.

Some said they can't afford to hire extra staff to do these jobs. But others actually find it difficult to delegate jobs to other people.

However, while some workers are worried about the rise of AI, some small business owners told Talker Research that AI software is helping them to get their time back.

It has also given them the confidence to do jobs that are new to them or they're not qualified to do.

The survey was commissioned by Adobe Express. Parimal Deshpande from the company said that AI has become really important for people running businesses. He said: "Small business owners have always had to do it all — but for the first time, they don't have to do it alone."
