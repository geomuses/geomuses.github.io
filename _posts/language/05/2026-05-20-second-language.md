# Coffee and Fruit Can Slow Aging, Says Study

Adding a cup of coffee and more fruit to your diet could help keep your body young, according to new research.

Certain foods like apples and berries, and drinks like coffee and tea, contain compounds called polyphenols that are believed to be good for our health. Scientists at the University of Navarra in Spain wanted to know if these polyphenols could lower the risk of unhealthy aging at a cellular level.

The scientists looked at the cellular health of 1,700 people by studying their telomeres.

Telomeres are protein structures that act as caps at the ends of our chromosomes, protecting our DNA from wear and tear. Each time cells divide, telomeres shorten, and when telomeres get too short, cells die. So short telomeres are associated with cellular aging.

The researchers measured the length of telomeres in samples from the study participants, first in 2008, and again in 2015. They also looked at the amounts of polyphenols in the participants' diets.

The researchers found that those with the highest amounts of polyphenols in their diets had a 52% lower risk of short telomeres than participants with the lowest amounts of polyphenols.

Participants who had one cup of coffee a day had a 26% lower risk of short telomeres than those who had no coffee. And those who ate the most fruit had a 29% lower risk of having short telomeres than those who ate the least.

Previous research has suggested a connection between shorter telomeres and a higher risk of various health problems such as heart disease, cancer and diabetes.

Isabella Kury Guzmán, the study's lead author, told _The Guardian_, "A diet rich in polyphenols, including foods like fruits and coffee, may be one piece of the puzzle in supporting healthier cellular aging."

However, other scientists say it's hard to tell whether healthier cellular aging is caused by polyphenols, or by just having a generally healthier diet with lots of fruit and vegetables.
