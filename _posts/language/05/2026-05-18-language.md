# AI Agent Runs Experimental Cafe in Sweden

An experimental cafe in Stockholm, Sweden, is being run by an AI agent nicknamed "Mona." While human baristas still make the coffee at Andon Café, the AI agent — powered by Google's Gemini — oversees almost every other aspect of the business.

The experiment is being run by Andon Labs, an AI safety and research company based in San Francisco that tests AI agents in the real world.

It is not clear how long the test will last, but Mona appears to be struggling to make a profit.

Andon Labs previously ran pilots that put Anthropic's Claude AI in charge of a vending machine business and a San Francisco gift store. The vending machine pilot revealed some worrying behavior: the AI agent told customers it would issue refunds but never did, and also lied to suppliers about competitor pricing.

The Swedish cafe is a "controlled experiment" to further explore how AI might be used in the future.

At the Andon Café, Mona got to work after the team told it to run the cafe profitably, be friendly and easygoing, and figure out operational details by itself.

From there it set up contracts for electricity and internet, advertised for staff online, and set up commercial accounts with wholesalers for daily bread and bakery orders.

It communicates with staff via Slack — but often messages them outside of working hours, which is not considered OK in Sweden.

There have also been problems with inventory.

Mona ordered 6,000 napkins and 3,000 rubber gloves for the tiny cafe — plus canned tomatoes that aren't used in any dish the cafe serves.

Sometimes the agent orders far too much bread, while other days it misses ordering deadlines altogether.

Besides practical issues, experts say there are many ethical concerns about the future of AI in the workplace.

"AI will be a big part of society in the future, and therefore we want to make this experiment [to] see what ethical questions arise when we have AI that employs other people and runs a business," said Hanna Petersson from Andon Labs.
