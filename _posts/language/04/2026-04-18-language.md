# What Your Emoji Say About Your Work

Have you ever added a smiley face to a work message?

Many people use emoji to make messages feel warm and friendly. But at work, they can sometimes send the wrong message.

A study from Canada's University of Ottawa looked at how emoji affect communication at work. In the study, over 200 adults read short work messages with different kinds of emotional tone.

Some messages were positive, some were negative, and some were neutral. Some of the messages had a positive "smiley face" emoji, others had a negative "angry face" emoji, and some had no emoji at all.

After reading the messages, participants answered questions about the sender, rating how competent they seemed and how appropriate the message felt for work.

In general, messages with no emoji were seen as more appropriate for work, and the sender was also seen as more competent.

Messages with a positive emoji were sometimes also judged well, especially when the message itself was positive or neutral. In these cases, the sender seemed a little warmer, and sometimes a little more competent too.

However, negative emoji had a negative effect. Messages with an angry emoji were often judged as less appropriate, and they also made the sender seem less competent.

This was especially true when the angry emoji was added to a neutral or positive message. The mismatch made the message feel strange and less professional.

The study found that positive emoji weren't always helpful, either. For example, when a positive emoji was used with a negative message, it could make the person seem insincere.

Emoji are more than just small pictures — they act like emotional signals. And in work messages, those signals can affect how people see your tone, how professional you look — and even how competent you seem!
