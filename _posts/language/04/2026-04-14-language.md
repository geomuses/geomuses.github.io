# How to Start Exercising and Make It a Habit

We all know that getting exercise is really good for our health. But it can be difficult to begin exercising — or to start exercising again after a break.

So what can you do if you want to begin exercising?

Experts say the first thing you should do is to talk to your doctor, just to see if your body is OK to begin exercising.

Next, make a plan. Think about what you want to get from exercise. Maybe you have a goal — for example, you want to run 5 kilometers.

Health website Healthline says you should start with small goals. So don't start by going out and running 5 kilometers — just start by going for a long walk. Then, make a new goal and try to run for just 1 kilometer.

Healthline says it's also important to make exercise a habit. That means you should put it in your calendar, and make time for it — don't just do it "sometimes."

At the start, you don't need to exercise for a long time, and you don't need to exercise every day. Just choose a time and put it in your calendar.

But experts say you should also be kind to yourself. Sometimes you wake up and don't feel so good, or maybe you have a job you really need to do, so you can't exercise. That's okay — you can try again the next day!
